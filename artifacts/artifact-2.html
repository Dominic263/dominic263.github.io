The Evolution of AI — A Visual Timeline

Introduction: An original, interactive visual timeline tracing seventy-five years of artificial intelligence — from Turing's founding question through two AI winters to the deep learning revolution and today's generative AI race.

Description

This artifact is a designed, scrolling timeline of AI history, created as an original web page and hosted within this portfolio. Events are color-coded into six eras — Foundations, the AI Winters, the Expert Systems Boom, Quiet Progress, the Deep Learning Era, and the Generative AI Era — so a viewer can see at a glance not just what happened, but the rhythm of boom and bust that shaped the field. Each milestone names the key figures and companies involved, and the two AI winters are presented as full-width "cold" bands explaining their causes and impacts.

Objective

Created for Workshop One of AIML-500 (Discussion 1.3: AI & ML Timelines), the objective was to research, design, and present a comprehensive timeline of AI's evolution — identifying major milestones from the 1950s onward, marking and explaining the AI winters, and highlighting the recent advancements driven by deep learning and cloud computing, with a focus on the major players and their contributions.

Process

  1. Research: I studied the three assigned sources — a documentary tracing AI's full history, the IEEE Computer Society's framework of AI "ages" from symbolic AI to connectionism, and Our World in Data's analysis of exponential growth in training compute — and organized the milestones they emphasized in common.
  2. Curation: I selected eighteen milestones that tell a coherent story: founding optimism, the mathematical limits that triggered the first winter, the expert-systems boom and second winter, and the hardware-plus-data convergence (Nvidia GPUs, ImageNet, the cloud) behind the deep learning and generative eras.
  3. Design: Rather than use a timeline generator, I designed the visual myself as a hand-coded web page: a color-coded era system, an alternating two-column layout on a central spine, "cold band" treatments for the winters, and scroll-triggered animations.
  4. AI assistance (disclosed): Per the assignment's guidance, I used AI tools (Anthropic's Claude) to assist with research synthesis and page development, then cross-checked every event against the assigned sources. The curation, story structure, and design direction are my own.

Tools & Technologies Used

  • HTML & CSS (hand-coded custom design — no timeline template)
  • JavaScript (IntersectionObserver scroll animations)
  • GitHub Pages (hosting, so the timeline is publicly shareable)
  • Claude (AI research assistant — used with disclosure per assignment guidelines)

Value Proposition

Unique Value

Where most timeline assignments use a drag-and-drop generator, this one is an original piece of software — researched, structured, and built from scratch. It demonstrates that I can take unstructured historical research and turn it into a designed, working product: the same synthesis-to-system skill I bring to engineering and physics research, applied here to communicating ideas.

Relevance

Understanding why AI winters happened — hype outrunning hardware, brittle systems that couldn't scale — is directly relevant to anyone deploying AI today. This timeline gives classmates, employers, and other learners a fast, memorable map of the field's history, and a cautionary framework for evaluating today's claims.
